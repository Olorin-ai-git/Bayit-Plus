## Messaging Pillars (Foundation)

### Pillar 1: Results-Driven ("Show Me the Money")
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- Quantify everything (%, $, time)
- Include context (customer size, industry)
- Provide attribution (customer name optional, industry required)

**Example:**
❌ "95% detection accuracy"
✅ "$XXX,XXX in fraud prevented per month for typical e-commerce platform"

### Pillar 2: Enterprise-Grade ("Trust Me")
- Certifications prominent (SOC 2, ISO, GDPR)
- Scale indicators ($ processed, customers, uptime)
- Third-party validation (audit, case studies)
- Security/compliance emphasis

**Example:**
❌ "Secure and compliant"
✅ "SOC 2 Type II certified. AES-256 encryption. Processing $XXB+ in annual transactions"

### Pillar 3: Speed & Efficiency ("Time is Money")
- Latency metrics (<100ms)
- Implementation speed (4 weeks)
- Operational savings (X% manual review time reduction)
- Decision time (real-time fraud blocking)

**Example:**
❌ "Fast fraud detection"
✅ "Detect and block fraud in <100ms. Deploy in 4 weeks. Save 60% on analyst review time"

### Pillar 4: Low Risk ("This Will Work")
- Customer success stories (case studies)
- Industry presence (vertical examples)
- SLA guarantees (99.99% uptime)
- Transparent implementation (4-phase guide)

**Example:**
❌ "Easy to implement"
✅ "Proven 4-week implementation. 99.99% uptime SLA. Typical results: 40% fraud reduction, 70% fewer false positives"

## Copy Tone Guidelines

### What to Remove
- ❌ Jargon without context ("multi-signal aggregation")
- ❌ Hype ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge")
- ❌ Vagueness ("advanced," "powerful," "intelligent")
- ❌ Unsupported claims ("best in class," "industry leading")

### What to Keep/Add
- ✅ Specificity (95%, <100ms, $XXM)
- ✅ Attribution (customer-validated, SOC 2 audited)
- ✅ Outcomes (fraud reduction %, savings $, time saved)
- ✅ Context (enterprise scale, "typical" results for their size)

### Tone Recipe
- Professional but approachable
- Specific, not generic
- Outcome-focused, not feature-focused
- Results-driven, not hype-driven
- Transparent about variability ("typical results")
- Enterprise-grade confidence
